Students will be able to

  • Identify common cyber security risks in everyday digital life using basic Chinese.
  • Ask and answer questions in Chinese about safe and unsafe online behaviours.
  • Compare online habits with others and discuss safe choices in an age-appropriate way.

Students with prior learning

  • Use wider vocabulary and sentence structures to explain online safety rules.
  • Describe and justify safe online practices with sequencing and cause-effect language.
  • Write a short reflective text or scenario response in Chinese.

Additional language learners

  • Use key vocabulary and sentence patterns to identify basic cyber safety ideas.
  • Respond to simple questions with sentence frames and visual support.
  • Participate in guided role-plays to practise safe online behaviours.
Learning Map

Four eSafety Learning Routes

Based on eSafety's "Be an eSafe Kid", discussion prompts are turned into classroom pathways.

01

Be Secure Protect Accounts and Information

  • What is a password? Why should we not tell other people?
  • Why should we avoid birthdays or 1234 as passwords?
  • Why should we pause and think before clicking links?
02

Be Safe Stay Safe Online

  • What situations can make you feel unsafe?
  • What can you do if something feels strange?
  • What online actions can help protect you?
03

Be Curious Curiosity and Thinking

  • Is everything we see online true?
  • Are strange messages or prize offers always real?
  • Should we copy popular online behaviour without thinking?
04

Be Kind Kind and Respectful Communication

  • What does kind language sound like?
  • Is it okay to insult people online? Why?
  • What can you do if you see someone being bullied?

Teaching Sequence

Classroom Activity Sequence

Each activity moves through judging safety, explaining reasons, and responding in Chinese.

01 Lead-in: Can / Cannot Circle and cross gesture check

Students use their arms to make a circle for "can / safe" and an X for "cannot / dangerous". The teacher reads online behaviours, such as sharing passwords, asking the teacher, clicking a message, and telling a stranger your name. Students judge each action and explain why.

Key Chinese
can, cannot, safe, dangerous, ask the teacher, protect privacy
Evidence of learning
Students can use gestures and simple sentences to explain whether an action is safe.
02 Vocabulary Building Sentence-making, matching, and scenario language

Students use vocabulary cards for matching, sentence making, and quick revision. They place words such as password, stranger, click, share, and privacy into sentence frames for scenario discussion.

Student task
Create a sentence with one keyword and one frame, for example: "You cannot share passwords."
Support
Provide sentence frames, visuals, and peer modelling.
03 Mini Scenarios Small group cyber safety decisions

Small groups draw scenario cards, such as "someone asks for your name" or "you receive a link from a stranger". Students discuss what they would do, use target vocabulary and sentence frames, then share with the class.

A stranger sends a link

I should not click it. I can ask my teacher.

A friend asks for your password

I cannot share my password. A password is private.

Someone is rude in a game

I should tell my teacher or family.

04 Listening & Speaking Password, personal information, trust, and help-seeking

Password Protection

Would you share your password with anyone other than your family? Why or why not?

Personal Information

What information should you never share online: address, school, phone number?

Online Trust

Can you trust new friends you meet online? Why or why not?

If Problems Happen

If you encounter something unsafe online, who would you ask for help?

05 Writing Practice 2-4 Chinese sentences from a cyber safety situation

Give students a short scenario and ask them to write 2-4 Chinese sentences explaining what they would do. Scenarios may include a link from a stranger, hurtful words in a game, or a friend asking for a password.

Example: I cannot click a stranger's link. I should ask my teacher. This is safer.

06 Group Project Create a 3-5 minute cybersecurity video

Student groups create a short cybersecurity video showing a common risk, safety advice in Chinese, and ways to ask for help. The video can be a role-play, interview, rule explainer, or storybook adaptation.

Success markers
Clearly present the risk, use target vocabulary, give a safe choice, and identify a trusted helper.
Teaching Skills

Teaching Skills

Internal notes for turning cybersecurity knowledge into speakable, performable, and reviewable Chinese classroom tasks.

front_hand

Gesture First, Language Second

Use "can / cannot" gestures to establish judgement, then ask students to add one Chinese reason.

record_voice_over

Keep Frames Short

Repeat compact frames such as "I can...", "I should not...", and "This is safer."

groups_2

Discuss in Groups First

Let students try scenario cards in small groups before sharing, reducing the pressure of speaking to the whole class.

movie

Use Structure for Videos

Student videos should include the risk, a Chinese safety rule, a helper, and a clear ending.

Cultural Component

Cross-cultural Comparison

Compare common apps, online rules, internet language, and abbreviations in China and Australia to discuss safe choices across digital cultures.
